Breast cancer brain metastases are a deadly sequela of primary breast tumors that overexpress human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2); median survival for patients with these tumors is 10 to 13 months from the time of diagnosis. Current treatments for HER2-positive breast cancer brain metastases are invasive, toxic, and largely ineffective. Here, we have developed an adeno-associated virus serotype 9 (AAV9) vector to express the anti-HER2 monoclonal antibody trastuzumab (Herceptin)
